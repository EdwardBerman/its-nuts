I'm an undergraduate at Northeastern University double-majoring in Mathematics and Applied Physics. I'm interested in building differentiable software for Cosmology, meaning my work is somewhere in the intersection of instrumentation and numeric methods. I am very active in the Julia community and I also like to dabble with Large Language Models from time to time with GammaTau AI. I'm grateful to have worked in Jacqueline McCleary's group at Northeastern University for the last several years and to be a part of COSMOS-Web: The James Webb Space Telescope Cosmic Origins Survey. Starting in July, I will be starting as a Network Science Institute research co-op working on a project with The Machine Intelligence Group for the betterment of Health and the Environment and the Social Urban Networks Lab.

Efficient PSF Modeling with ShOpt.jl: A PSF Benchmarking Study with JWST NIRCam Imaging

Edward Berman, Jacqueline McCleary, Anton M. Koekemoer, Maximilien Franco, Nicole E. Drakos, Daizhong Liu, James W. Nightingale, Marko Shuntov, Diana Scognamiglio, Richard Massey, Guillaume Mahler, Henry Joy McCracken, Brant E. Robertson, Andreas L. Faisst, Caitlin M. Casey, Jeyhan S. Kartaltepe. In submission to the Astronomical Journal.

We propose new methods for empirical point spread function characterization and implement them in Julia. We benchmark our PSF fitter against PIFF and PSFex and show that our approach bridges the accuracy and features of PIFF with the speed of PSFex.

ShOpt.jl : A Julia Package for Empirical Point Spread Function Characterization of JWST NIRCam Data

Edward Berman, Jacqueline McCleary. In submission to the Journal of Open Source Software, AAS Companion Paper.

Companion paper to the full AAS submission, details some of the more intricate software decisions made in developing our PSF fitter ShOpt.jl.

Can It Edit? Evaluating the Ability of Large Language Models to Follow Code Editing Instructions

Federico Cassano, Luisa Li, Akul Sethi, Noah Shinn, Abby Brennan-Jones, Jacob Ginesin, Edward Berman, George Chakhnashvili, Anton Lozhkov, Carolyn Jane Anderson, Arjun Guha. In submission to the first annual Conference on Language Modeling, 2024.

We develop benchmarks to evaluate large language models on code editing performance, as previous benchmarks proved insufficient. We also fine-tune models for specifically code editing.

Reflexion: Language Agents with Verbal Reinforcement Learning

Noah Shinn, Federico Cassano, Edward Berman, Ashwin Gopinath, Karthik Narasimhan, Shunyu Yao. arXiv preprint.

We show that giving Large Language Models an Episodic memory buffer results in state of the art performance on a wide variety of benchmarks, including HumanEval (programming), Alfworld (decision making), and HotpotQA (reseaoning). We release Leetcode Hard Gym, a programming benchmark for LLM agents.
